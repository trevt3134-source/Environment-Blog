🌡️ The Causes of Global Warming

Understanding the greenhouse gases driving our planet's temperature rise

The Greenhouse Effect is the major cause of global warming and this is caused by the presence of gases such as carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O) that trap heat within the atmosphere of the earth. Human activities are the major cause of these emissions namely combustion of fossil fuels to generate electricity and transport, industrial production, and massive deforestation process that destroys the trees that normally absorb carbon.

With such gases, the average temperature of the planet increases causing a change in the natural environment, which was a very delicate balance in terms of thermal regulation to the civilization that must now contend with a rapidly warming world. The first approach to solving the issue of energy consumption and to proclaim the necessity of the transition to renewable energy sources is awareness of these drivers.

Key Facts 📊

  • Global temperatures rising ~1.1°C since pre-industrial times
  • CO₂ levels highest in 3 million years
  • Fossil fuels account for 75% of emissions

🌊 Climate Change and Its Effect

How changing climate patterns impact our world and vulnerable populations

Climate change is characterized as the changes in temperatures and weather patterns over a long period of time with the effect that it brings with it more and more frequent and severe natural disasters such as super typhoons, extended droughts and catastrophic flooding.

To society, these effects will be food insecurity as crops will not grow, there will be displacement of communities living near seashores because of rising sea levels and the high health risks caused by extreme heat waves. Melting glaciers and warming oceans are destroying crucial habitats in the natural environment, driving a number of species to the verge of extinction.

In this case, it is vital to promote sustainability and should we make our infrastructure smart and minimize our environmental footprint so that the most vulnerable groups are not exposed to such life-changing changes.

🦁 Impact of Biodiversity Loss

Preserving the diversity of life for ecosystem health and human survival

Loss of biodiversity takes place in the cases when the ecosystem is polluted, overexploited and habitat destroyed, and there is a domino effect whereby the extinction of one species causes a subsequent extinction of another. This loss compromises the natural environment to offer necessary services in which the society depends, e.g. clean water filtration, insect pollination of crops, and finding new medicines in plants.

Going into extinction is robbing us of our natural shield against climate change and outbreaks of diseases when we lose biodiversity, and thus environmental protection is a question of human existence. It has become our duty to help conserve what remains of the "hotspots" of life and preserve it to provide a stable and functioning planet to the generations to come.

⚠️ Did You Know? More than 1 million species are at risk of extinction due to human activities. Every species plays a role in maintaining ecological balance.

♻️ Philippines Waste Management

Tackling plastic pollution and building a sustainable waste management system

Waste management in the Philippines is another key problem that has the sachet economy and so much single-use plastics in the landfill or the open ocean. The mismanagement of the waste blocks urban drainage systems consequently causing devastating flooding in the cities and leaks toxic chemicals into the soil and water causing harm to the people and sea life in our archipelago.

Although there are laws such as the RA 9003, it is upon the duty of each citizen to engage in proper segregation and aid in initiatives by the local government to enhance a better collection of waste. Creating awareness about the life of our trash would help us bring about the change towards the circular economy where waste is perceived as a resource as opposed to a burden on our beautiful islands.
